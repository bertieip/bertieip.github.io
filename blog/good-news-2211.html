Egg whites could be used to filter microplastics from seawater

Who would have thought that the eggs we eat at breakfast could be the key to cleaning up microplastics in the ocean?

Researchers at Princeton University have experimented with many different ingredients to try and recreate a perfect aerogel structure.

The specific structure they were looking for creates a lightweight porous material which can remove contaminants from water without releasing chemical residue.

The research showed that pure protein in the egg whites was able to create this structure better than any other ingredient they had experimented with which interconnected strands of carbon fibres and sheets of graphene aerogel.

In the future, this groundbreaking research could be used in many types of applications, including water filtration, energy storage, and insulation.

EU toughens rules for members‚Äô greenhouse gas emissions

With greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions increasingly contributing to climate change, the European Union (EU) has reached a provisional agreement to tackle this issue.

The Council of the European Union and the European Parliament, which represents the 27 EU members, have reached an agreement that addresses targets to mandatorily toughen the reduction of EU countries' GHG emissions.

Across sectors such as transport, agriculture, buildings, and more, the new regulations indicate that EU members must reduce their emissions by 40% by 2030.

Under the new regulation, it is proposed that individual member states will each have a specific national target which ranges from 10-50%.

In addition, each member state will also have its own linear emission trajectories, these will need to be continuously regulated so that the confirmed GHG emission levels are not exceeded.
