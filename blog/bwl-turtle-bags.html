Turtle Bags is on a mission to raise awareness surrounding the amount of plastic waste in the ocean which is extremely harmful to aquatic life.

They also work closely with charities such as the Marine Conservation Society to help maintain marine biodiversity and prevent ocean pollution and climate change.

We caught up with Beth Williams, Founder of Turtle Bags, to discuss how they are changing the world of textiles in the hopes of reducing marine plastics.

Why does Turtle Bags exist?

Over 14 million tons of plastic waste is released into the ocean each year which makes up 80% of the debris found in marine environments. This form of pollution threatens marine ecosystems, our food safety, human health, and leads to climate change. Most marine species will ingest plastic over their lifetimes or can end up entangled in plastic waste, which can eventually cause severe injuries and death.

‚ÄúI'm an ecologist and I knew that not only plastic was getting out into the seas in a way that it shouldn't, but also beginning to move through the food chain and harming marine biodiversity.

My mission was to set up a business that would raise awareness about marine plastics and offer an alternative to plastic everyday essentials which is sustainably and ethically sourced.‚Äù

What are the key things Turtle Bags does best?

Small company with a big voice

It is a devastating fact that over half of the 300 million tonnes of plastic produced each year makes single-use products such as shopping bags, cups and straws. Therefore, Turtle Bags wants to raise awareness by providing alternative reusable products which can be used in a variety of ways. The company offers a range of reusable bags that can hold up to 40kg of cargo. The bags can be used as grocery, produce, and string bags.
