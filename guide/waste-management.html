Onboarding Guide: Waste Management

Getting Started

You have been selected as the data holder for Waste Management in the office. As such, we need you to complete the template we have provided.

This guide will help you to complete the data into the format needed to build this part of the company impact.

Finding and Requesting Data

Waste Management is usually handled by an external company, and they quite often track waste very accurately. As such, if you use a supplier like First Mile then you may be able to access very accurate data. You can use the template below to request this information, or else you may be able to log-in to an online platform if this is provided.

Template If you need to request the data you can use the following email template. Simply copy and paste into your email client and delete sections as appropriate.

Dear [data holder],
We're undertaking an analysis of our carbon impact and need some information about our office.
If you could provide me the following information for the period of [start date to end date], I'd be grateful:

General Waste
  • Usage: The total waste in kg for each month
  • Handled Method: Whether this goes to Landfill, Energy-from-Waste facilties, or is handled with another method?
Recycling
  • Usage: The total recycling in kg for each month
Organic Waste
  • Usage: The total waste in kg for each month
  • Handled Method: Whether this goes to Landfill, Anaerobic Digestion facilities, or is handled with another method?
Many thanks, and please let me know if you have any questions!


If this isn't possible, then you have two options:

  1. A weekly tracking exercise: In this case you can track waste for a week and then use this for the whole year. To do this, you need to keep a tally of how much waste/recycling is removed during the week.
  2. An estimation: Alternatively, you can simply Unknown? = TRUE for that office location.

In general, we are asking you to build a picture of your consumption rather than needing surgical precision.

Completing Data Template

The Waste Management Template

Admin Data

The orange panel on the left hand side of the sheet gives you all your admin data.

Admin Data

  • Basic Data: This is the company name and the period of data analysis
  • Location Data: This is the location of offices to complete data input for

Progress Data

  • Progress: here you can track the progress of the data collection
  • Completed By: Once this data has been completed, add your email and completion data so that we can ask any questions upon verfication.


Waste Management Data

The locations and addresses will copy over automatically into the Office Location column. All you need to do is fill in the usage data and the supplementary data associated with it.

For each item type, enter the units of waste creation during each month in question, for each office location. If information is unknown for any location you can select Unknown? = TRUE for that office location.

For each type, you can also enter the Main Supplier if known. This gives us information on the status of your Environmentally Preferred Purchasing strategy.

  • General Waste: This is waste that goes in the trash. It either ends up at landfill (yuk!) or goes to an Energy-from-Waste facility. Your Facilities Management Team will be able to give you details of the contractor who holds this information.
  • Recycling: This is waste that is re-used, typically in a closed-loop (turned back into the same thing).
  • Food and Organic Waste: Organic waste is important because if it goes to landfill it emits huge amounts of methane, so it is preferable to handle it via an anerobic digestion facility.

Frequently Asked Questions


What if we're in a WeWork or similar?

You should ask the management who handles waste in the building because this will give some information about what happens to the waste.

For the values we recommend using a standard value of 1kg per person per day for recyling and waste, and 0.5 kg for organic/food waste. This is likely an overestimation, but gives the company a benchmark to make future improvements.
