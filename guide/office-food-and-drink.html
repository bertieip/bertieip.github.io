Onboarding Guide: Office Food and Drink

Getting Started

You have been selected as the Office Food and Drink data holder. As such, we need you to complete the wizard for in the Reporting Period.

You'll see the following task in your to-do list here: vso.alectro.io/todo-list

This guide will help you to complete the data into the format needed to build this part of the company impact.

Finding and Requesting Data

As most information is internal, you should be able to complete the template from your own systems/knowledge, without requiring external assistance.

In general, we are asking you to build a picture of your consumption rather than needing surgical precision.

That being said, often the easiest place to find this information is on receipts/invoices from supplier.

Completing the Data

We have provided a template that you can download to collect the data before uploading it if required.

Click here to download the template

When you click to begin your task, you'll see the Food and Drink onboarding panel.

Step 1: Complete the Wizard

Click Start Wizard to enter the data

Select the location you're entering data for to Start. There may be separate data-holders per location, but please check with your admin if you're unsure.

Answer the quick questions about whether the item is provided in the office and about your supplier:

  • Is the item provided in the location? Tick the box if the item is provided in the office
  • Which company provides the item? Tell us who provides the item. We can make suggestions if the supplier isn't aligned with the company's net zero goals. Don't worry if you don't have this information, it can be entered or updated later on.
  • Is this supplier an EPP choice? Has this supplier been chosen with Environmentally Preferred Purchasing in mind? Again, don't worry if you don't have this information, it can be entered or updated later on.

Complete the four pages of quick questions and click 'Submit'

If you need to complete the wizard for another location, you can select it here, or else head back to the wizard homepage by clicking Done.

Step 2: Tweak your data

Now you've completed the initial wizards you can tweak the data for each individual month

Click on the greyed out tick mark for each location to bring up the edit modal

From here you can adjust each month by changing the number in the functional units.

Data is typically reported in the following units

  • Dairy Milk: The easiest way to report this is how many litres are purchased monthly (this information can come from invoices if delivered, or from office knowledge if purchased locally)
  • Non-dairy Milk: Like dairy milk, this is usually purchased as a litre, and should be reported monthly.
  • Coffee: This should be reported in terms of total kilograms if ordered in bags/brought at the shop. If you use Nespresso pods or similar, conver the number of pods into kg by using the appropriate conversion factor. Typically, 1 pod = 5g (0.005 kg)
  • Tea: This is usually easiest to report because the company knows the rough number of tea bags it goes through per month. A tea bag typically weighs 2g (i.e. 1 Tea Bag = 0.002 kg)
  • Wine: If the company provides wine in the office then you can add these here. This is counted by the bottle.
  • Beer: If the company provides beers in the office then you can add these here. This is counted by the bottle.
  • Soft Drinks: If the company provides soft drinks in the office then you can add these here. This is a typical can of soft drink.
  • Fruit: You can add office supplied fruit here with an approximation portions. For example, a banana or apple is 1 portion, as is a bunch of grapes is 1 portion. Each would be entered as a single item in the input.
  • General Office Groceries: If the company ourchases snacks, crisps, biscuits etc, you can anter these here. Enter the typical monthly spend on these.

Once you've made your edits, check the box on the right hand side, and click to 'Confirm Data'.

Step 3: Mark as Complete

The grey check mark will now have turned green for each completed location. Once all locations are completed, you can mark the whole section as complete by clicking 'Mark as Complete'

The section will complete, and you can go back to your to-do list if you have other items to complete, else your work is done!

Your to-do list will now have a completed message in it.


Frequently Asked Questions


What if I don't know the data?

We are trying to get an idea of consumption rather than needing a forensic examination of the food and drink you consume in the office. Therefore, you should be able to accurately estimate any data that isn't accessble.

If data really isn't available, then keep an eye on a typical week and then extrapolate for the whole year to get a good estimate from a real-world example


What if we're in a WeWork or similar?

Free coffee! Nice! In this case, its best to roughly estimate how many coffees are drunk in a typical week and to then copy this across all months. The other good news is that the coffee WeWork uses is carbon-neutral - free, and guilt free!


What if we buy snacks that don't fall into the categories?

For biscuits, chocolate, nuts, fruits, and crisps, this will typically cover all office snacks. If you order snacks that don't quite fall into the category you can select the category you think suits them best, or get in touch and we'll help you to categorise them.

The same can be said for other items, so please get in touch if you need a hand!
