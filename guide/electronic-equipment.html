Onboarding Guide: Electronic Equipment

Getting Started

You have been selected as the data holder for electronic equipment. As such, we need you to complete the template we have provided.

This guide will help you to complete the data into the format needed to build this part of the company impact.

Finding and Requesting Data

By electronic equipment we are referring to the equipment contained in your asset list, or similar items. We are not referring to ad-hoc items purchased as these will be covered in expense data.

This will include items like your Desktops, Laptops, Monitors, Tablets, Mobile Phones, Servers, and TVs.

We will use this data to calculate the embodied emissions of the equipment (Scope 3), taking into account the depreciation periods of the equipment.

This data will either be internal, or requestable by your tech provider if this is external.


Completing Data Template


Admin Data

The orange panel on the left hand side of the sheet gives you all your admin data.

Admin Data

  • Basic Data: This is the company name and the period of data analysis

Progress Data

  • Progress: here you can track the progress of the data collection
  • Completed By: Once this data has been completed, add your email and completion data so that we can ask any questions upon verfication.


Electronics Data

All you need to do is fill in the yellow boxes, providing any supplementary data associated with it, and we'll do the rest.

Item Data
  • ID: typically, each asset list has an id associated with it to identify it.
  • Category: this is the type of item with options for Desktop, Laptop, Tablet, Mobile Phone, Server, or TV. If your item is not listed, you can simply add it in and we'll do the rest.
  • Make: this is the make of the item, for example Apple or Dell.
  • Model: this is the model of the item, for example Macbook Pro (for Apple) or XPS 13 (for Dell).
  • Date Purchased: This is the purchase/lease start date for the item.

Frequently Asked Questions


What if we don't have an asset list?

If you don't have an asset list, we recommend creating one. They are beneficial for tax related depreciations, and also for reducing the overall impact over time.

If you're creating this manually, we recommend making your best guess at how many items you have based on individual team member items, and the total number of employees. This will give a good estimation in the first year of analysis, and then can be used in subsequent years as the basis for the asset list.


What if we don't know purchase dates?

If this is the case, use the first day of the analysis as a default value.
