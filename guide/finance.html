Onboarding Guide: Expense Data

Getting Started

You have been selected as the data holder for Expense Data. As such, we need you to upload the expenses so that we can analyse the data.

This guide will help you to complete the data into the format needed to build this part of the company impact.

Finding and Requesting Data

Typically expenses should be available as a direct download or export from an expense software.

Please complete this through a direct .xls extraction from your CRM or Expense System.

Data should be on a line-by-line basis per transaction.

The more descriptions per expense item the better it is for the analysis.

Completing Data Template


Admin Data

The orange panel on the left hand side of the sheet gives you all your admin data.

Admin Data

  • Basic Data: This is the company name and the period of data analysis

Progress Data

  • Progress: here you can track the progress of the data collection
  • Completed By: Once this data has been completed, add your email and completion data so that we can ask any questions upon verfication.


Expense Data

This guide will walk you through the information we require from the expenses

  • Expense Reference: Simply identifier from your expense system e.g. 00001
  • Account Category: Usually your cost centre e.g. Sales Team, or HR
  • Project Code (if applicable): Helps us to categorise by client projects if required
  • Date of Purchase: The transation date.
  • Expense Description: A plaintext description of the item e.g. "lunch at Pizza Express for 4 people"
  • Expense Category 1: The top level categorisation
  • Expense Category 1: A supplementary category.
  • Supplier Name: The supplier e.g. British Airways.
  • Currency: The currency e.g. USD or GBP.
  • Debit The total spend.
  • Credit: The total credit if a refund etc.
  • Net: Total cost (positive or negative)

Frequently Asked Questions


What if I don't know the data?

Expense data is crucial for the analysis to take place. Without spend data, there is no way to quantify the material impact of the company over the year. That being said, there is also no way to do traditional financial accounting without this spend data being tracked! Therefore, every single organisation needs this data for accounting purposes, and so it should be available within the company.
