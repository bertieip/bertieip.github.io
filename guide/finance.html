Onboarding Guide: Expense Data

Getting Started

You have been selected as the data holder for Expense Data. As such, we need you to upload the expenses so that we can analyse the data.

You'll see the following task in your to-do list here: vso.alectro.io/todo-list

This guide will help you to complete the data into the format needed to build this part of the company impact.

Finding and Requesting Data

By ExpensesÂ we mean the company's general expenditures throughout the year. This is where we get information on things like business travel and flights, meals and subsistence, other purchased items (i.e. amazon spend, gifts, stationary, office furniture), and anything else that isn't covered in one of the other data upload categories.

Ideally, these expenses should be available as a direct download or export from your expense software, but some organisations might hold this information in a simple Excel (or similar) format - either is fine.

Each transaction should be represented as a separate line item, and the more detailed descriptions you can provide for each expense, the better it will be for our analysis.

What to include in the Expense data upload

Regardless of whether you use the template, or simply upload raw export data, this guide will walk you through the information we require from the expenses:

Category Description Required
Reference A unique identifier generated by your expense system, such as "2023_00001" Yes
Date The specific date on which the transaction occurred. Yes
Description A top level, clear and concise written description of the item or expense, for example, "lunch at Pizza Express for 4 people." Yes
Category 1 The primary or top-level categorization, often corresponding to the Nominal Code name, used to classify the expense. Yes
Category 2 An additional categorization or supplementary information, which can include details like the supplier's name or a subcategory. Optional
Spend (GBP) The total cost of the transaction, which can be either a positive or negative value and should encompass all expenses, including any Value Added Tax (VAT) if applicable. Yes

In some cases, you may wish to include the following categories for more information:

  • Currency: The currency e.g. USD, GBP, EUR - we will assume GBP unless otherwise told.

Finally, depending on the detail of your expenses, the following special categories can be used to split the end carbon emissions further:

  • Location ID: If expenses are associated with specific locations, please provide the Location ID as obtained from the template download step.
  • Department Code: Typically, this represents your cost center, such as Sales Team or HR, indicating the department or team responsible for the expense.
  • Project Code: If applicable, this code helps categorize expenses related to specific client projects, facilitating project-based accounting and tracking.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I don't know the data?

Expense data is crucial for the analysis to take place. Without spend data, there is no way to quantify the material impact of the company over the year. That being said, there is also no way to do traditional financial accounting without this spend data being tracked! Therefore, every single organisation needs this data for accounting purposes, and so it should be available within the company.


What data should be included?

At a top level, anything that the business spends during the year could contribute to the carbon impact. However, items like taxes, payroll, bank fees, revaluations, and depreciations can be exluded.
